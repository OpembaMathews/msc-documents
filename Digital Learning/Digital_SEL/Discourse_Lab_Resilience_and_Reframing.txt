2️⃣ Theoretical Explanation: SEL and Cognitive Reframing
In the VE5914701 curriculum, resilience is framed through the CASEL Core Competency of Self-Management, specifically the ability to regulate emotions and thoughts in stressful digital contexts. Cognitive Reframing serves as a vital SEL tool that bridges the gap between a stressor (such as "Technostress" or "Information Overload") and a positive well-being outcome. By applying the PERMA-V model (Positive Emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, Achievement, and Vitality), reframing allows a learner to transform a negative digital experience into an opportunity for growth. Instead of a fixed mindset that views digital disruption as a threat to "Social Coherence," reframing encourages a growth mindset where the learner actively evaluates "Digital Affordances" to find meaning. This process is central to "Digital Agency," where the student moves from being a passive recipient of algorithmic influence to an active participant in their own emotional regulation. This meaningful adaptation ensures that the "Virtual Microsystem" remains a site of flourishing rather than exhaustion, promoting long-term "Social Actualization" within the learning community.

4️⃣ Personal Reflection
In our Digital SEL Discourse Laboratory, I’ve noticed how my emotional state fluctuates based on my "Social Integration" within Moodle. Initially, when I couldn't find "Meaning" in the sheer volume of peer posts, I felt overwhelmed—a clear sign of "Technostress." Using the SEL framework of Self-Management, I reframed this challenge: instead of seeing it as a burden of "Information Overload," I viewed it as a rich "Social-Emotional Asset" where I could practice "Empathy" and "Relational Mapping." This shift in mindset changed my "Narrative Identity" within the course from a struggling student to a "Digital Steward." By intentionally focusing on the "Quality of Interaction" rather than just the quantity of content, I felt a renewed sense of "Social Contribution." This cognitive reframing didn't just reduce my stress; it enhanced my "Vitality" and helped me achieve the "Social Acceptance" necessary for a successful collaborative learning experience.
